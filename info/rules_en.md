## 1) Objective

Win by having the most Victory Points (VP) at game end.

Endgame is triggered when:

- any player reaches 10+ VP, **or**
- the Conflict deck is empty.

Finish the current round, then determine the winner.

Tiebreakers (in order):

1. Spice
2. Solari
3. Water
4. Troops in garrison

If still tied (very rare), FAQ final tiebreaker: the player who most recently took a Reveal turn wins.

---

## 2) Core Uprising Concepts

- Hybrid game: worker placement + deck building.
- Each round has 5 phases:
  1. Round Start
  2. Player Turns (Agent/Reveal)
  3. Combat
  4. Makers
  5. Recall
- Uprising has no Mentat.
- Major Uprising systems:
  - `Spies`
  - `Sandworms`
  - `Shield Wall` token
  - `Maker Hooks`
  - `Battle icons` on Conflict/Objectives

---

## 3) Setup (Base Uprising)

1. Place the board on the Uprising side.
2. Put the `Shield Wall` token in its marked setup area.
3. Place 4 Alliance tokens on faction tracks.
4. Build the Conflict deck:
   - 4 Conflict III cards on bottom,
   - 5 random Conflict II cards (out of 9) above them,
   - 1 random Conflict I card (out of 3) on top.
5. Prepare card areas:
   - Shuffle Intrigue deck.
   - Shuffle Imperium deck, reveal 5 cards as the Imperium Row.
   - Place reserve stacks: `Prepare the Way` and `The Spice Must Flow`.
6. Each player:
   - chooses a Leader,
   - takes a 10-card starting deck and shuffles it,
   - takes 1 water,
   - takes all player components (agents, control markers, cubes, spies, etc.).
7. Create resource bank (Solari, spice, water, preferred sandworms, Maker Hooks).
8. Prepare Objectives by player count, deal one to each player, assign First Player marker.

Note: 1/2/6-player games use official supplemental setup/rules.

---

## 4) Factions, Influence, and Alliances

- Sending an Agent to a faction space usually:
  - resolves the board space effect,
  - increases Influence with that faction by 1.
- At 2 Influence: gain 1 VP.
- At 4 Influence: gain that track's bonus.
- First player to reach 4 Influence with a faction takes that faction's Alliance token (+1 VP).
- If another player passes you on that track, Alliance transfers.
- FAQ: if you drop to 3 Influence or lower, you lose that Alliance.

## 5) Round Structure

### 5.1 Phase 1: Round Start

- Reveal the top Conflict card.
- Each player draws up to 5 cards for the round.

### 5.2 Phase 2: Player Turns

Players alternate clockwise until everyone has taken a Reveal turn.

On your turn, choose:

- `Agent turn`, or
- `Reveal turn` (you may do this early even with agents remaining).

#### Agent turn

1. Play 1 card with a valid Agent icon.
2. Send 1 Agent to a legal board space (or an occupied one via Spy Infiltrate).
3. Pay mandatory board-space costs.
4. Resolve effects:
   - board space,
   - card Agent-box,
   - faction Influence gain (if applicable).

Unless a rule/effect says otherwise, you may choose the order of these effects.

Restrictions:

- one card sends only one agent;
- cards with no Agent icon cannot be played on an Agent turn;
- if you cannot pay mandatory cost immediately, you cannot go there.

#### Reveal turn

1. Reveal all remaining cards in hand.
2. Resolve Reveal-box effects in any order.
3. Spend persuasion to acquire cards.
4. Set/update combat strength on the Combat track.
5. Clean up cards to discard.

#### Acquiring cards

- You may acquire from Imperium Row or reserve.
- Acquired cards go to discard pile.
- Imperium Row is always refilled to 5.
- Unused persuasion is lost at end of Reveal turn.

## 6) Units, Combat Strength, and Deployment

### 6.1 Troops

- Recruiting a troop moves it from supply to garrison.
- Troops help in combat only if deployed to the Conflict area.
- When sending an Agent to a Combat space, you may deploy:
  - any troops recruited **that turn** from any source, plus
  - up to 2 troops from your garrison.

FAQ clarification: you cannot bypass the "up to 2 from garrison" limit by re-recruiting garrisoned troops.

### 6.2 Sandworms

- When an effect says "summon sandworm":
  - take one from bank and deploy directly to Conflict;
  - sandworms never enter garrison.
- Usually requires having `Maker Hooks`.
- FAQ: `Maker Hooks` are not spent when summoning.
- You cannot summon sandworms to protected critical-location conflicts while Shield Wall remains.

### 6.3 Strength values

- Each troop in Conflict = 2 strength.
- Each sandworm in Conflict = 3 strength.
- Each sword from revealed cards = +1 strength.
- If you have no units in Conflict, your strength is 0 (even with swords).

## 7) Critical Locations and Shield Wall

Critical locations:

- Arrakeen
- Spice Refinery
- Imperial Basin

When a Conflict for one of these locations is won:

- winner places a control marker there;
- that player gets bonus whenever any player sends an Agent there
  (normally +1 Solari for Arrakeen/Spice Refinery, +1 spice for Imperial Basin).

While `Shield Wall` is intact:

- sandworms cannot be summoned to conflicts at those 3 locations.

When a Shield Wall destruction icon appears:

- FAQ: destruction is optional;
- once removed, it is gone for the rest of the game;
- sandworms may then be summoned to any conflict.

## 8) Spies

Spies are placed on observation posts.

- On Spy icon gain, place a Spy from supply to an unoccupied post.
- If no Spy is in supply, you may recall one first (no effect) in order to place.

Main uses:

1. `Infiltrate`  
   Recall your connected Spy to send your Agent to a space occupied by another player's Agent.

2. `Gather Intelligence`  
   When sending an Agent, recall a connected Spy to draw a card.  
   Decision timing: immediately after placing your Agent, before resolving board/card effects.

3. Spy Agent icon  
   Lets you send an Agent to connected spaces without recalling the Spy.

FAQ limits:

- You still must play a card and perform an Agent turn to recall a Spy for Infiltrate/Gather Intelligence.
- You may recall two Spies in one turn only if effects are different (one for Infiltrate, one for Gather Intelligence).

---

## 9) Intrigue Cards

Types:

- `Plot` — playable during your Agent or Reveal turns.
- `Combat` — playable only during Combat phase.
- `Endgame` — playable only at game end.

General rules:

- You must meet conditions and pay costs to play an Intrigue card.
- FAQ: if Intrigue deck is exhausted, shuffle discarded Intrigue cards into a new deck.
- FAQ: if you gain a "when you win a conflict" Intrigue as a conflict reward, you may play it immediately.

---

## 10) Phase 3: Combat

1. Starting with First Player, each player with at least one unit in Conflict may play any number of Combat Intrigues or pass.
2. Once all relevant players pass consecutively, resolve combat.
3. Rewards are granted by final strength ranking (1st, 2nd, and 3rd for 4/6-player cases).
4. Winner takes the Conflict card into supply.
5. Check battle icon match:
   - if winner has another face-up Conflict/Objective with same icon, flip both face down and gain 1 VP.
6. Return all troops from Conflict to owners' supply; return all sandworms to bank.
7. Reset Combat markers to 0.

### Ties

- Tie for 1st: tied players each gain 2nd reward; no one wins/takes the Conflict card.
- Tie for 2nd: tied players each gain 3rd reward.
- Tie for 3rd: no reward.

### Sandworm reward doubling

If you have at least one sandworm in Conflict when taking rewards, your rewards are doubled.

Not doubled:

- taking location control,
- battle icon matching from won Conflict cards.

If reward has "pay X to gain Y," you may pay that cost a second time to gain it a second time.

---

## 11) Phase 4: Makers

Check maker spaces (typically in Uprising:
`Deep Desert`, `Hagga Basin`, `Imperial Basin`; in 6-player also `Habbanya Erg`):

- if no agent is on the space, add 1 spice to that space's bonus spice;
- bonus spice accumulates across rounds.

---

## 12) Phase 5: Recall

- All players recall agents.
- Pass First Player marker clockwise.
- If endgame not triggered, begin a new round.

---

## 13) Endgame

1. Players may play Endgame Intrigue cards.
2. Compare VP totals.
3. Apply tiebreakers.

FAQ:

- Players can exceed 12 VP even though score track usually ends at 12.

---

## 14) CHOAM Module (in Uprising box)

In short:

- Adds contracts and extra cards.
- Without module: contract icon gives 2 Solari.
- With module: contract icon lets you take one face-up contract.
- FAQ: completing a contract is mandatory once its conditions are met.
- Multiple contracts tied to the same board space can all complete from one Agent placement to that space.

Recommended onboarding: play at least one game without CHOAM first.

## 15) Common Misplays (Official Rulings)

1. **Shield Wall icon** — destroying Shield Wall is optional; if removed, it never returns.
2. **Maker Hooks** — not spent when summoning sandworms.
3. **Spies** — Infiltrate/Gather Intelligence still requires a normal Agent turn.
4. **Intrigue deck empty** — reshuffle discarded Intrigues to form a new deck.
5. **When you win a Conflict Intrigue** — can be played immediately if drawn as conflict reward.
6. **0 units in Conflict** — strength is 0 even if swords are present.
7. **Deploy cap** — at most 2 troops from garrison when sending an Agent to a Combat space.
8. **The Spice Must Flow VP** — VP from acquiring it is kept even if card is later trashed.

---

## 16) Quick Order-of-Resolution for Edge Cases

Use this order when timing/effects become unclear:

1. Identify current phase/timing window.
2. Check hard constraints in text (`must`, `only`, `when`).
3. Pay mandatory board-space costs first.
4. Resolve board and Agent-box effects in chosen order (unless fixed by text).
5. For Intrigues, validate type timing (Plot/Combat/Endgame) and play conditions.
6. For Gather Intelligence, decide recall immediately after Agent placement.
7. For sandworms, verify Maker Hooks requirement and Shield Wall status.
8. If interpretations conflict, default to official FAQ/Errata.

---

## 17) Compatibility Note

Uprising is compatible with earlier Dune: Imperium products. When combining with `Rise of Ix` and/or `Immortality`, follow official Uprising combination instructions (overlay behavior, card/space exceptions, conflict recommendations, and specific card interactions).
